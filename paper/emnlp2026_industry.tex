\title{Perceptually Better, Semantically Worse:\\
Measuring Speech Enhancement Impact on
LLM-Based Voice Systems}

% Anonymous submission
% \author{...}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
Speech enhancement (SE) is applied as a universal preprocessing step
in spoken AI pipelines under the assumption that better audio quality
improves downstream task performance.
We introduce \textbf{Output Divergence Rate (ODR)}, which measures
how often SE changes an LLM's intent classification relative to
clean speech, and evaluate five enhancement conditions on the SLURP
test set (2{,}974 clips) through Whisper large-v3 and wav2vec2-large
cascades.
Every condition produces significant ODR ($p < 0.001$); MetricGAN{+}
more than doubles ODR versus unenhanced noisy speech (0.318 vs.\
0.135) despite improving PESQ, and unmitigated echo reaches ODR of
0.836 through a qualitatively distinct failure where ASR transcribes
the wrong speaker entirely.
Standard quality metrics show at best moderate correlation with ODR
(PESQ: $\rho\!=\!-0.467$; SQUIM-MOS: $\rho\!=\!-0.068$), and both
findings replicate across ASR architectures, indicating that current
perceptual evaluation tools are insufficient proxies for LLM pipeline
quality.
\end{abstract}
 
%% ============================================================
\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
%% ============================================================
 
Modern spoken AI systems chain three components: speech enhancement
(SE) $\rightarrow$ automatic speech recognition (ASR) $\rightarrow$
large language model (LLM).
The SE step is assumed acoustically neutral, yet recent evidence
challenges this at the transcription level: \citet{chondhekar2025denoising}
show MetricGAN{+} increases semWER across all 40 tested ASR
configurations, and \citet{islam2026denoising} report the same for
diffusion-based SE.
What remains unknown is whether these distortions propagate to LLM
reasoning.
This is not obvious: LLMs exhibit robustness to noisy
text~\citep{chen2026voicebench}, so transcription degradation may not
translate to semantic task failure, or it may cause systematically
different failures that WER cannot measure.
 
We introduce \textbf{Output Divergence Rate (ODR)}, the first metric
measuring SE effects on LLM semantic output, and construct a
diagnostic benchmark across five enhancement conditions on
SLURP~\citep{bastianelli2020slurp}.
Results reveal a nuanced picture: LLMs show partial robustness to
spectral distortion (WER--ODR gap $< 0$ for all conditions), yet echo
conditions produce catastrophic ODR through a qualitatively distinct
speaker-substitution failure.
Critically, standard audio quality metrics do not predict this damage,
and findings are consistent across two architecturally distinct ASR
models, ruling out Whisper-specific artefacts.
 
\noindent\textbf{Contributions:}
\begin{enumerate}
\item ODR metric and five-condition benchmark on SLURP (2{,}974 clips,
  77 intents). Pipeline, enhanced
  audio, and evaluation scripts released at
  \url{https://anonymous.4open.science/r/se-llm-odr-benchmark}.
\item All conditions produce significant ODR; MetricGAN{+} more than
  doubles noisy ODR (0.318 vs.\ 0.135) despite improving PESQ.
\item Systematic intent collapse: MetricGAN{+} biases divergent clips
  toward catch-all categories (\emph{general\_quirky},
  \emph{general\_greet}), a distributional shift invisible to WER.
\item Quality metrics show a pooled correlation with ODR
  (PESQ $\rho\!=\!-0.467$) that is driven primarily by
  between-condition separation: within any single condition,
  no metric exceeds $|\rho|\!=\!0.34$, showing perceptual
  scores cannot predict \emph{which} clips will diverge
  within a given SE pipeline.
\item Findings replicate with wav2vec2-large (identical ranking;
  higher absolute ODR, mean $\Delta\!=\!+0.157$): results are
  architecture-agnostic but suggest CTC-based systems face greater
  enhancement-induced damage.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}
%% ============================================================
 
\paragraph{SE and ASR.}
SE-induced ASR degradation is well-documented:
\citet{lee2023d4am} propose joint SE+ASR training to mitigate it;
\citet{chondhekar2025denoising} show MetricGAN{+} increases semWER
across all tested configurations; \citet{islam2026denoising} extend
this to diffusion-based SE.
Our work takes the next step: measuring whether these distortions
propagate to LLM semantic output.
 
\paragraph{SE and downstream NLP.}
\citet{rai2024denoasr} show denoising amplifies demographic bias in
ASR.
\citet{ali2022time} propose joint SE+intent training, acknowledging
that independently optimised SE may not serve downstream NLP;
however, their approach requires retraining, whereas ours diagnoses
the effect in existing deployed pipelines.
 
\paragraph{Spoken LLM evaluation.}
VoiceBench~\citep{chen2026voicebench} evaluates LLM voice assistants
under acoustic variation but does not isolate the SE preprocessing
step.
The URGENT challenge~\citep{zhang2024urgent} benchmarks SE using WER
and perceptual metrics with no downstream NLP track.
ODR fills this gap.

\section{Benchmark Design}
\label{sec:method}
%% ============================================================
 
\subsection{Dataset and Conditions}
 
We use the \texttt{slurp\_real} test split of
SLURP~\citep{bastianelli2020slurp}: 2{,}974 real human recordings
across 18 domains and 77 intent classes.
The TTS subset is excluded as its uniform acoustics suppress
enhancement artefacts.
Table~\ref{tab:conditions} summarises the five enhancement conditions,
chosen to cover the major SE classes deployed in commercial spoken AI.
\input{tables/table1_conditions}

\textbf{Noisy} adds DNS Challenge noise~\citep{reddy2021interspeech} at
SNR$\,=\,$10\,dB without enhancement, establishing a degraded
baseline.
\textbf{MetricGAN{+}}~\citep{fu2021metricgan+} applies discriminative
GAN-based noise suppression trained to maximise PESQ, representative
of the most widely deployed SE class.
\textbf{Echo~(sim)} convolves a DNS far-end signal with a room
impulse response at $\text{SER}\!\in\!\{-10,\,-5,\,0\}$\,dB (ITU-T
P.1110) without cancellation, isolating a conferencing-specific
failure mode absent from prior NLP benchmarks.
\textbf{Dereverberation} applies WPE~\citep{nakatani2010speech} to
reverberant speech ($\text{RT60}\!\in\!\{0.3,\,0.6,\,0.9\}$\,s).

\textbf{Echo + DTLN-AEC.}
We additionally evaluate a complete AEC pipeline by
applying DTLN-AEC~\citep{westhausen2021acoustic},
a dual-signal transformation LSTM network for acoustic
echo cancellation, to the Echo~(sim) microphone
signal using the far-end reference signal.
This condition tests whether neural AEC restores LLM
performance degraded by unmitigated echo.
 
\subsection{Evaluation Pipeline}
 
Each clip is transcribed by two ASR models: Whisper
large-v3~\citep{radford2023robust} (encoder-decoder, beam size 5,
temperature 0) as the primary model, and wav2vec2-large-960h~\citep{baevski2020wav2vec}
(self-supervised CTC) as the architectural comparison.
Transcripts are classified by Gemini~2.5 Flash Lite via the Gemini
API using a fixed closed-set prompt over the 77 SLURP intents
(temperature 0, max 15 tokens; see Appendix~\ref{app:prompt}).
 
ODR for condition $C$ is:
\begin{equation}
\text{ODR}(C) = \frac{|\{i : \hat{y}_C^{(i)} \neq
\hat{y}_{\text{clean}}^{(i)}\}|}{N}
\label{eq:odr}
\end{equation}
where $\hat{y}_C^{(i)}$ is the predicted intent under condition $C$,
$\hat{y}_{\text{clean}}^{(i)}$ is the clean baseline, and $N$
excludes INVALID predictions.
We additionally report the WER--ODR gap:
\begin{equation}
\text{Gap}(C) = \text{ODR}(C) - \min(\overline{\text{WER}}_C,\,1.0)
\label{eq:gap}
\end{equation}
A negative gap indicates the LLM is partially robust to the
transcription errors induced by that condition.
We report 95\,\% bootstrap CIs ($B\!=\!10{,}000$, seed$\,=\,$42).
 
\subsection{Quality Metrics}
 
We compute six metrics spanning intrusive perceptual (PESQ, STOI),
intrusive signal-level (SNR, SI-SDR), and non-intrusive MOS
prediction families (SQUIM-MOS, SRMR), and report Spearman $\rho$
and Pearson $r$ against binary ODR with Bonferroni correction
($n\!=\!6$).

\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}
%% ============================================================
 
\subsection{ODR Across Conditions}
 
Figure~\ref{fig:odr} reports ODR for both
ASR models across all non-clean conditions.
All conditions produce statistically significant ODR ($p\!<\!0.001$),
and the condition ranking is identical for both models:
Echo (sim) $>$ Echo + DTLN-AEC $>$ MetricGAN{+} $>$ Noisy $>$
Dereverb.
 
\begin{figure}[t]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\columnwidth]{figures/fig1_odr.pdf}
\caption{ODR per condition for Whisper large-v3 (dark blue,
with 95\,\% CI) and wav2vec2-large (light blue, with 95\,\%
CI). All conditions significant ($p\!<\!0.001$, binomial test).
Condition ranking is identical across architectures.
Whisper ODR: Echo (sim) 0.836, Echo + DTLN-AEC 0.404,
MetricGAN{+} 0.318, Noisy 0.135, Dereverb 0.108.
wav2vec2 ODR: 0.853 / 0.587 / 0.474 / 0.352 / 0.320.}
\label{fig:odr}
\end{figure}
 
Under Whisper, MetricGAN{+} produces ODR of 0.318
(CI\,[0.301,\,0.335]), more than doubling the unenhanced noisy
baseline (0.135, CI\,[0.123,\,0.147]).
This is the central finding: MetricGAN{+} substantially improves PESQ
and STOI yet introduces additional LLM semantic damage beyond what
noise alone causes, demonstrating that perceptual quality improvement
and LLM task performance are not aligned.
Dereverberation produces the lowest ODR (0.108,
CI\,[0.097,\,0.119]), comparable to the noisy baseline.
Under wav2vec2 the same ordering holds at higher absolute values
(MetricGAN{+}: 0.474; noisy: 0.352), consistent with CTC decoding's
greater sensitivity to frame-level spectral artefacts.

\input{tables/table2_metrics}

Table~\ref{tab:metrics} quantifies the
perceptual--semantic decoupling.
MetricGAN{+} raises PESQ from 1.76 to 1.94
relative to Noisy ($\Delta\!=\!+0.18$), a modest
improvement, while simultaneously \emph{reducing}
STOI from 0.87 to 0.80 ($\Delta\!=\!-0.07$), and
more than doubling ODR from 0.135 to 0.318.
This triple divergence is particularly damaging for
practitioners: the metric that MetricGAN{+} is
explicitly trained to maximise (PESQ) improves, the
intelligibility proxy (STOI) worsens, and the
downstream LLM task failure rate doubles.
No single quality metric provides a reliable signal
about the direction of LLM task impact.
 
\paragraph{WER--ODR gap.}
Figure~\ref{fig:wer_gap} plots WER against ODR for both models.
All eight points fall below the diagonal ($y\!=\!x$): the LLM is
partially robust to transcription errors in every condition and for
both architectures, with Whisper gaps ranging from $-0.092$ to
$-0.400$ and wav2vec2 gaps from $-0.125$ to $-0.152$.
For Echo (sim), ODR of 0.836 and capped WER of 0.928 yield a
gap of $-0.092$, reflecting WER saturation rather than LLM robustness: with raw
WER of 1.448, Whisper transcribes the far-end loudspeaker rather than
the user, producing a valid but entirely wrong transcript.
 
\begin{figure}[t]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\columnwidth]{figures/fig2_wer_gap.pdf}
\caption{WER vs.\ ODR for Whisper (filled markers) and wav2vec2 (open markers).
Each condition has a unique marker shape.
All points fall below $y\!=\!x$ (dashed), indicating partial LLM
robustness to transcription errors across all conditions and
architectures.}
\label{fig:wer_gap}
\end{figure}
 
\paragraph{Echo condition.}
The echo condition produces ODR of 0.836 (CI\,[0.823,\,0.849]) under
Whisper and 0.853 under wav2vec2, converging across architectures.
At $\text{SER}\!\leq\!0$\,dB, both models transcribe the louder
far-end echo signal rather than the near-end speaker.
This is a categorically different failure from spectral distortion:
the LLM processes a semantically unrelated utterance, and WER cannot
characterise the damage because it saturates.

\paragraph{Echo with cancellation.}
Applying DTLN-AEC neural echo cancellation to the
Echo~(sim) signal substantially recovers ASR
performance: mean WER drops from 1.448 to 0.700
($-51.6\,\%$ relative; full WER distribution in
Appendix~\ref{app:wer}), confirming that the echo
canceller correctly suppresses the dominant far-end
signal and restores near-end speech transcription.
The downstream LLM impact follows proportionally:
ODR drops from 0.836 to 0.404
(CI\,[0.386,\,0.422], Gap\,=\,$-0.245$),
a $51.7\,\%$ relative reduction that closely mirrors
the $51.6\,\%$ WER reduction.
This proportionality suggests that the
speaker-substitution artefact is the primary damage
mechanism for echo: once AEC restores the correct
speaker, both transcription and intent recovery
improve at nearly the same rate.
 
\paragraph{Systematic intent collapse.}
Of 945 diverged clips under MetricGAN{+}, a disproportionate fraction
collapse to catch-all categories (\emph{general\_quirky},
\emph{general\_greet}),
revealing distributional shift in LLM input space: SE removes
domain-specific lexical cues rather than adding random noise.
Table~\ref{tab:examples} shows representative examples of this
collapse.

\subsection{Quality Metrics as ODR Predictors}
 
Figure~\ref{fig:correlations} reports Pearson $r$ (left) and
Spearman $\rho$ (right) for each metric against both WER and ODR.
 
\begin{figure*}[t]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.48\linewidth]{figures/fig3a_pearson.pdf}\hfill
\includegraphics[width=0.48\linewidth]{figures/fig3b_spearman.pdf}
\caption{Pearson $r$ (left) and Spearman $\rho$ (right) of each
quality metric against WER (blue) and ODR (orange). PESQ and STOI
correlate comparably with both targets; non-intrusive SQUIM-MOS is
near-zero for both, underscoring its inability to predict either
transcription or semantic damage. All correlations significant
($p\!<\!0.001$, Bonferroni corrected).}
\label{fig:correlations}
\end{figure*}
 
PESQ achieves the strongest Spearman correlation with ODR
($\rho\!=\!-0.467$), accounting for
$\approx\!22\%$ of ODR variance, a moderate relationship at best.
PESQ and STOI correlate comparably with WER and ODR
($\rho_{\text{WER}}\!=\!-0.439$ vs.\ $\rho_{\text{ODR}}\!=\!-0.467$
for PESQ), confirming that perceptual metrics track transcription
and semantic damage with similar (moderate) strength.
SQUIM-MOS, the most attractive non-intrusive estimator for production
monitoring (it requires no reference signal), is near-zero for both
($\rho_{\text{WER}}\!=\!-0.069$, $\rho_{\text{ODR}}\!=\!-0.068$),
ruling it out as a lightweight proxy for either target.
For echo specifically, AECMOS echo\_mos, a metric designed for
AEC quality assessment, achieves only $\rho\!=\!-0.080$ against
ODR, suggesting that even purpose-built echo quality metrics do not
reliably predict LLM task failure.

\paragraph{Pooled correlations overstate predictive value.}
The correlations above pool clips across all conditions, conflating
within-condition variation with between-condition separation:
Echo (sim) simultaneously has the lowest mean PESQ and the
highest ODR as a \emph{condition}, which alone can produce a
substantial pooled correlation even if PESQ does not distinguish
diverged from non-diverged clips \emph{within} any single condition.
Recomputing each metric's correlation separately within each
condition yields $|\rho| \leq 0.34$ (vs.\ the pooled
$\rho\!=\!-0.467$), indicating that no metric provides meaningful
signal about \emph{which} clips will diverge once the enhancement
condition is fixed.
This sharpens our central claim: practitioners cannot use PESQ (or
any of the six metrics tested) to anticipate per-clip LLM failures
within a given SE pipeline; the pooled correlation reflects
coarse condition-level separation, not clip-level predictive power.
 
\subsection{Architecture Robustness}
 
Table~\ref{tab:model_comparison} (Appendix~\ref{app:arch}) confirms
that findings are not Whisper-specific: wav2vec2 produces the
identical condition ranking (Spearman $\rho\!=\!1.0$, $p\!<\!0.001$)
with higher absolute ODR on spectral
distortion conditions (mean $\Delta\!=\!+0.157$).
The sole exception is Echo (sim), where both models converge
($\Delta\!=\!+0.017$): speaker-substitution failure is
architecture-agnostic.
The higher wav2vec2 ODR on spectral conditions suggests that
CTC-based systems, common in latency-constrained deployments,
may face greater enhancement-induced LLM damage than encoder-decoder
estimates indicate.
 
\subsection{Domain-Level Vulnerability}
 
ODR varies across SLURP domains (Table~\ref{tab:domain_odr}):
short formulaic commands
(\emph{lists}: mean ODR 0.419, \emph{alarm}: 0.386) are more
vulnerable than open-ended domains (\emph{news}: 0.319,
\emph{qa}: 0.315), as formulaic utterances have less lexical
redundancy for the LLM to recover intent from a degraded transcript.
Of 4{,}149 divergent clips, 90.4\% represent domain-level shifts;
only 9.6\% are within-domain action confusion, confirming that SE
distortion destroys coarse domain signals rather than producing
fine-grained intent errors.

\section{Deployment Implications}
\label{sec:implications}
%% ============================================================
 
\paragraph{WER is an insufficient SE evaluation metric.}
Our results show WER can overestimate LLM damage (MetricGAN{+}:
WER\,=\,0.647, ODR\,=\,0.318, Gap\,=\,$-0.296$) or saturate while
ODR remains catastrophic (Echo (sim): WER\,=\,1.448,
ODR\,=\,0.836).
We recommend ODR alongside WER as a standard evaluation criterion
for any SE front-end deployed before an LLM, with particular urgency
for CTC-based ASR systems where our results suggest ODR is
systematically higher.
 
\paragraph{Echo cancellation is a hard requirement.}
The echo condition reaches ODR of 0.836 and 0.853 under both ASR
models: the spoken AI system correctly handles fewer than one in six
user interactions.
DNSMOS and PESQ score the near-end signal characteristics and cannot
detect this failure, because they do not assess which speaker was
transcribed.
In any conferencing deployment, functional AEC must be treated as a
prerequisite for LLM integration, not a quality optimisation.
 
\paragraph{Perceptual metrics cannot replace LLM task evaluation.}
PESQ, the direct training objective of MetricGAN{+}, explains
only 22\% of ODR variance; non-intrusive estimators explain under 1\%.
SE pipelines optimised for perceptual quality scores provide no
reliable guarantee of downstream LLM performance.
ODR measurement on a representative intent task should be included
in SE evaluation protocols for spoken AI systems.

\section*{Limitations}
%% ============================================================
 
\textbf{Simulated conditions.}
All degradations are simulated using DNS Challenge corpora; real
device conditions may differ in acoustic profile and complexity.
 
\textbf{ASR scope.}
We test encoder-decoder (Whisper) and CTC (wav2vec2) architectures;
streaming CTC and attention-CTC hybrid models remain untested.
 
\textbf{English only.}
SLURP is English-only; enhancement effects may differ for tonal or
morphologically complex languages.
 
\textbf{Closed-set classification.}
ODR is defined over a fixed 77-class intent vocabulary; open-ended
generation tasks may show different sensitivity.
 
\textbf{Single LLM.}
We use Gemini~2.5 Flash Lite; larger or task-specialised LLMs may
show different robustness profiles.
 
\textbf{AEC scope.}
The echo condition includes both uncancelled echo and a single
neural AEC system (DTLN-AEC); other AEC architectures and
multi-microphone setups remain untested.

\section*{Ethical Considerations}
This work uses publicly available datasets (SLURP,
DNS Challenge) and publicly available models
(Whisper, MetricGAN{+}, WPE, wav2vec2). No
personally identifiable information was collected
or generated. The SLURP dataset was collected with
informed consent from participants. Our findings
expose a potential reliability failure in deployed
spoken AI systems; we believe responsible
disclosure of such failures serves the public
interest by enabling practitioners to make
informed deployment decisions.
